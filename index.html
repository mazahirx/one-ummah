Understanding Muharram and What do shia practices

Why Do Shia Muslims Observe Muharram?

Muharram, especially the 10th day known as Ashura, is deeply significant to Shia Muslims. It marks the martyrdom of Imam Hussain (RA), the grandson of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, at the Battle of Karbala. Shia Muslims observe Muharram to honor his sacrifice for justice, truth, and Islam. They mourn through processions, recitations, and charity such as setting up Sabeels (free water stations).

Purpose of This Website

This platform is created to promote inter-sect understanding and clarify misconceptions about Shia beliefs and their observance of Muharram. It supports peaceful learning, unity, and mutual respect.

The Importance of One Ummah

Islam emphasizes unity and brotherhood. Despite our differences, all Muslims believe in the oneness of Allah, the finality of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, and the guidance of the Qur'an. Muharram reminds us not only of historical events, but also of the need to remain united as one Ummah — respectful of our diversity and committed to peace and justice.

"Indeed this Ummah of yours is one Ummah, and I am your Lord, so worship Me." — Surah Al-Anbiya (21:92)

Q&A - Clarifying Common Questions

Q: Do Shias believe in a different Quran?

A: No. Shias believe in the same Quran as Sunni Muslims. The text of the Quran is identical for all Muslims.

Q: Why do Shias do matam (self-flagellation)?

A: It is a symbolic expression of grief and solidarity with the suffering of Imam Hussain (RA). Not all Shias perform it, and many scholars discourage the extreme forms.

Q: Do Shias insult the Sahaba (companions)?

A: Shia theology emphasizes respect for the Prophet’s family (Ahlul Bayt), and while there are historical differences of opinion, most Shias do not engage in disrespect toward the Sahaba.

Q: Why do Shias commemorate events so emotionally?

A: The tragedy of Karbala is considered a deeply emotional moment in Islamic history. The mourning expresses love and loyalty to the Prophet’s family.
